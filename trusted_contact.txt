
🎯 Purpose of Trusted Contacts

Trusted contacts are people the user appoints to act as witnesses, verifiers, or guardians of their posthumous letters. These people help ensure that the delivery of letters is appropriate, secure, and intentional — especially when it depends on sensitive conditions, like the death of the user.

⸻

🧩 Key Functions of Trusted Contacts
	1.	Verification of Death
Contacts can be prompted to confirm whether the user is deceased before letters are sent. This adds a human layer to a digital trigger (like inactivity).
	2.	Manual Approval or Cancellation
Higher-trust contacts may be allowed to approve, delay, or even cancel the sending of a letter.
	3.	Backup Recipients
In case a primary recipient is unreachable or inactive, a trusted contact can receive the letter instead.
	4.	Security & Consent Layer
Prevents unauthorized or premature release of sensitive content.

⸻

🧭 User Workflow

1. Adding a Trusted Contact
	•	User navigates to a dedicated “Trusted Contacts” section in their dashboard.
	•	They click “Add Contact”.
	•	A form appears asking for:
	•	Full name
	•	Email address (mandatory)
	•	Phone number (optional)
	•	Relationship (e.g. spouse, friend, sibling, lawyer)
	•	Trust level (view-only, confirm death, cancel letter)

2. Contact Confirmation
	•	Once submitted, an email is sent to the contact:
“You’ve been added as a Trusted Contact by [User Name]. Click here to confirm.”
	•	The contact must click to confirm they consent to this role. Until then, they remain “unconfirmed” and cannot act.

3. Managing Contacts
	•	User can:
	•	View a list of added contacts
	•	See who’s confirmed
	•	Resend confirmation links
	•	Edit their details and permissions
	•	Remove them at any time

⸻

🔐 Permissions & Roles (Optional)

Trusted contacts may have tiered responsibilities, for example:

Role	Description
Basic	Can be informed but take no action.
Verifier	Can confirm the user’s death.
Executor	Can approve, delay, or cancel delivery.
Backup	Receives letters if the original recipient fails.

You can allow the user to assign roles to each contact at the time of creation or editing.

⸻

🕒 Trigger Workflow

Here’s how trusted contacts are used at the time of letter delivery:
	1.	The app detects inactivity or a specific scheduled date.
	2.	It emails the user:
“Are you still alive? Confirm to prevent sending letters.”
	3.	If the user does not respond within a time window (e.g. 7 days), the system:
	•	Notifies the trusted contacts.
	•	Asks them to confirm or deny the user’s death.
	4.	Once a threshold (e.g. 2 of 3 contacts) confirms death:
	•	Letters are delivered.
	5.	If contacts deny or delay, delivery is paused.

⸻

✉️ Contact as a Backup Recipient (Optional)

You can allow the user to select any contact to:
	•	Receive a copy of the letter
	•	Be the sole recipient in case of bounced emails or failed delivery
	•	Access the letter in a secure dashboard with time-limited access

⸻

🛡️ Privacy and Consent Handling
	•	Trusted contacts must give explicit consent before being active.
	•	All data is encrypted and never shared publicly.
	•	The user can remove or modify their role at any time.
	•	Contacts can decline the role when first invited.

⸻

💡 Future Enhancements
	•	Multi-factor verification for contacts (e.g., SMS + email)
	•	Activity logs (e.g. contact X confirmed on date Y)
	•	Contact approval hierarchy (e.g., two of three contacts must approve)
	•	Smart reminders to user to update contacts annually

⸻

Summary

This feature brings peace of mind, safety, and dignity to the sensitive task of legacy letter delivery. It introduces real-world verification into a digital system and empowers users to ensure their final messages are handled respectfully.
